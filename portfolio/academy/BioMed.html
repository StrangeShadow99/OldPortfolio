Biomedical Academy

APPLY

Focuses on the foundations of a medical career

The Biomedical Sciences Academy is centered around the human body and its functions and diseases. The academy is held in the mornings and afternoons at the Edgewood Campus. Scholars in this academy will take one mathematics class, one science class, and one biomedical sciences class, taught by Brian Noll, Nicole Mathews, and Sara Anderson respectively. Students applying can expect to use a wide variety of laboratory equipment and perform year-long research and experiment projects.

Our Teachers

Nichole Mathews has been working in education for 16 years and with the Academies for two years. She currently teachers Accelerated Biology, Biology, Human Body Systems, and Biomedical Innovations. She holds a BS in Biology and Secondary Education, a MA in Reading and Writing, a Rank 1 in Special Education, and is certified to teach PLTW PBS, HBS, and BI. She loves teaching anything about the human body. When she isn't at school, she loves to spend time with her husband and four children. She loves the water, including boating, tubing, wakeboarding, and she loves the beach!

Sara Anderson began her education career in 2003 by teaching Biology 101 labs at her alma mater, Ohio State. This is her fifth year at the Biomedical Sciences Academy where she teaches advanced chemistry and courses from Project Lead the Way Biomedical Curriculum Principles of Biomedical Sciences and Medical Interventions. In addition to being certified to teach all four of PLTWs Biomedical courses, she is licensed to teach Biology and Chemistry, as well as the AP level of those subjects. After obtaining her BS in Biology from Ohio State, she went on to earn an MS in Secondary Education from Indiana University. She is currently finishing her EdS degree at Northern Kentucky University. Sara loves to teach her students that being a nerd is awesome, almost as awesome as genetics which is her favorite unit to teach. In her free time she enjoys playing music, baking, and being crafty.

Mr. Pelfrey has been teaching for 6 years. This is his 6th year with the Academies. He currently teaches Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2, and Pre-calculus in the Biomedical Sciences Academy. He holds B.S. in Mathematics/Secondary Education, M.A. in Mathematics, and Ed.S. in Educational Leadership. His favorite topics to teach are trigonometry and quadratics. Mr. Pelfrey has two year old boy/girl twins named Silas and Selah and a 2 month old baby boy named Amos. His favorite thing to do is spend time with my wife and kids!
