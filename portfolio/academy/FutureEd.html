Future Educators Academy

APPLY

Focuses on the foundations of a career in education

The Future Educators Academy is centered around the skills essential for a career in education. The academy is held in the afternoons at Scott HS. Scholars in this academy will take one mathematics class, one English class, and one education class, taught by Ms. Mckinley, Casey Wolfe, and Melissa Insko respectively. This academy is held in the mornings at Scott High School. Students interested in applying can expect a multitude of different projects to tackle throughout their high school career, most of which will be focused on the effectiveness of new and old teaching methods in the classroom.

Our Teachers

Melissa Insko is the Teaching Professions Instructor for the Future Educators Academy. In her four years with the academies, she has completed her Ed.S. degree in Educational Leadership, and helped to start three different academy programs. Currently, she teaches 'Education Basics,' 'Students Today,' 'Art of Teaching,' and an 'Education Capstone' course for seniors which includes a clinical experience component. Mrs. Insko also teaches two dual-credit courses through Northern Kentucky University— 'EDU 104: Orientation to the Education Program' and 'EDU 305: Introduction to Education'—and oversees three other online classes. Among her favorite topics to teach are 'History of Education in the US' and the various 'Educational Philosophers' who have shaped schools to the present day.Mrs. Insko and her husband, Matt, have been married for 17 years and reside in Ohio. They also have a 15-year-old daughter, Allison, who is a sophomore in high school. ring her spare time, Mrs. Insko is an avid reader and crafter, an active members in her Church, and a semi-professional ski jumper. Her longest jump of 177 meters set a hometown record in the wn where she grew up. Her favorite TV shows are Ken Burns' documentaries, and reality shows such as 'Swamp People' and 'Deadliest Catch.'

Casey Wolfe is in her third year of teaching English I, II, and III with the Future Educators and Computer Science Academies. She has a Bachelor's and Master's degree from UK in Secondary English Education. Additionally, she is certified to teach AP English and she is a Google Level 1 and 2 Certified Educator. She enjoys teaching writing to scholars so they can improve their presentations. She is currently watching The Office for the first time, so the the obsession with Dwight K. Schrute is starting to make sense! Her first baby will be born before the end of the school year!

Kristen McKinley joined the Academies in 2017. She is the mathematics teacher for the Future Educators Academy and the Informatics/Computer Science Academy. In the Academies, Kristen teaches Algebra 1, Algebra 2, Geometry, and Pre-Calculus. In college, Kristen danced for the National Champion University of Louisville Ladybirds Dance Team. Kristen holds a bachelors and a master’s degree in Secondary Math Education. Kristen started her teaching career in Indiana in 2001 teaching at New Albany High School. She taught there for ten years. While a teacher at New Albany, she coached the Bulldolls Dance Team. That team earned the titles 'State Champions' and “National Champions' under her direction. In 2009 she want the award 'Outstanding Supervising Teacher in Secondary Education' from Indiana University Southeast. Mrs. McKinley was named 'Teacher of the Year' for New Albany High School in 2010. She also sponsored the Math Team, Student Council, and was treasurer of the PTO. In 2011 Ms. McKinley and her family moved to New Mexico. During the six years there, she was a certified group fitness instructor, a cheer/dance coach, and the PTA President. In 2016, Kristens husband transferred to Cincinnati. Kristen enjoys cooking, crafting and spending time with her family (she has a daughter at Twenhofel Middle School and a son who attends Piner Elementary).
