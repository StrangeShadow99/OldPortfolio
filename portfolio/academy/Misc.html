Principal and Founder

Dr.Francis O'Hara

Dr. Francis O’Hara is the Director of the Kenton County Academies of Innovation and Technology and a pioneer in the field of career and technical education. His educational goals led him to develop two programs in the state of Kentucky that capture the attention and passion of the modern scholar. Dr. O’Hara focuses on the “Z” generation’s social behavior and technology skills in an effort to develop an educational experience that is relevant and meaningful. His passion and drive to have successful scholars led to the creation of the Academy concept. Dr. O’Hara earned his Bachelor, Master, and Doctorate degrees in Education from the University of Kentucky. Aside from the Academies, Dr. O’Hara is an established educator not only in Kentucky education, but also globally. Dr. O’Hara enjoys visiting educational systems across the world and has presented and studied systems in Finland, Africa, and Sweden. Outside school, Dr. O’Hara is a massive KISS fan and loves to ride his Harley Davidson motorcycle. Contact at:francis.ohara@kenton.kyschools.us

Principal

Dr.Julie Whitis

Ms. Julie Whitis is the Assistant Principal of the Kenton County Academies of Innovation and Technology and in her fourteenth year as an educator in the Kenton County School District. Ms. Whitis earned a Ba of Science degree in Biology and a Ma of Secondary Science Education degree from NKU. In addition, her second Ma degree is in Instructional Leadership from the U of the C. She taught Biology and Anatomy for ten years, and was the Science Department Chairperson at SK High School for five years before joining the Academy family. In addition, she was a Site-Based Council Member for six years. Contact at: julie.whitis@kenton.kyschools.us
