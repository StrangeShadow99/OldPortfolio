Womens' Engineering Academy

APPLY

Focuses on female empowerment in a male-dominated career path

The Women's Engineering Academy is focused on teaching female students the fundamental skills of a career in engineering to break the stereotype of a mans job. This academy is held in the afternoon from 11:35 to 2:10pm. Scholars in this academy will take one mathematics class, one english class, and one engineering class, taught by Alyssa Leimstoll, Ian Olano, and Adam Klaine respectively. Scholars experience the process of design and analysis in engineering including how to work effectively in teams, as well as developing skills in project management, engineering fundamentals, oral and graphical communication, logical thinking, and modern engineering tools

Our Teachers

Mr. Olano is in his third year of teaching high school math and his first year with the Academies. He graduated from Scott High School and earned his BA in Mathematics from U of L. He received a Masters of Education in Curriculum and Instruction from UC. He has a passion for the field of mathematics and a love for helping others, and takes personal pride in being a part of scholars' success. In his free time, he loves spending time with his family and his church, playing guitar, hunting, and playing soccer.

Ms. Alyssa Leimenstoll has been teaching English in the HPPT and Engineering Academies since 2013. She earned a BA degree in English with Secondary Teaching Certification from NKU and is currently attending there for her MA in Education with an endorsement in Instructional Computer Technology. She is certified to teach AP Language Arts and Composition. Her strengths include breaking down complex writing processes into simplified steps. She enjoys spending time outside, reading, and watching sports.

Mr. Adam Klaine has taught in the Academies since 2012, and currently teaches in both the Engineering and High Performance Production Technology Academies. He received a BS degree in industrial science from WKU and is currently working on his MA in Secondary Education there. He acquired years of architectural experience before teaching. One of his strengths is relating to scholars, as he has lived in this community his whole life. Mr. Klaines family is extremely important to him, and his hobbies include hunting, fishing, farming, carpentry and remodeling, and landscaping.
