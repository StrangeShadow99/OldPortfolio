Engineering Academy

APPLY

Focuses on the application of computer science and engineering to the creation of automated machines.

The goal of the Robotics Academy is to introduce students to the robotics aspect of engineering and the design process involved in that, by implementing sketches and CAD software into creating robots. The academy is held in the mornings at Simon Kenton HS. Scholars in this academy will take one mathematics class, one English class, and a Principles of Engineering class, taught by Ian Olano, Alyssa Leimenstoll, and Adam Klaine, respectively. Students applying can expect to use 3D printers and various CAD (computer animated design) programs and software. For projects, students will be expected to innovate a new product.

Our Teachers

Ian Olano is in his 4th year of teaching, his 2nd with the Academies. Mr. Olano has a BA degree in Mathematics from the University of Louisville and a M.Ed from the University of Cincinnati. He teaches Algebra 1, Algebra 2, Pre-calculus and AP Calculus AB to the scholars in the Engineering and Women's Engineering Academies. He has certifications in Google Level 1, PLTW Digital Electronics and AP Calculus. Mr. Olano's favorite math topics are Trigonometric Identities and Applications of Derivatives. In his free time, Mr. Olano enjoys spending time with his beautiful wife and two children, and he is (uncontroversially) the reigning champion of the KCAIT Staff Fantasy Football League.

Ms. Leimenstoll has been teaching for five years and started her career in the Academies. She teaches Accelerated English 1, Accelerated English 2, English 3, and AP Language and Composition in the Engineering and Women's Engineering Academies. She earned her BA in Secondary English Education and her MAED from Northern Kentucky University. Ms. Leimenstoll has also completed Project Lead the Way's Computer Integrated Manufacturing Training and Google Level 1 Certification. With the rest of the Engineering Academy team, she co-coaches the Academies' VEX Robotics Teams. Finding cross-curricular connections with Engineering topics and curriculum is one of her favorite parts of teaching in the Academies. Outside of school, Ms. Leimenstoll is an avid sports fan (especially soccer).

Adam Klaine has been teaching for eight years and working in the Academies for six years. In the Engineering Academy he teachers Introduction to Engineering and Design, Civil Engineering and Architecture, Principles of Engineering, Computer Integrated Manufacturing, AeroSpace Engineering, Digital Electronics, and Engineering Design and Development. Mr. Klaine holds a B.S. in Industrial Engineering and a MA in Education as a Teacher Leader. His favorite lessons to teach are on Inventor and Revit software, architectural model making, basic robotics construction, robotic arm control, glider design, bread boarding, and A3 problem solving. When he is not at school, he loves watching his two daughters play soccer and ride bikes. He loves to spend any of his free time in the woods hiking with his family or on the water.

Laurie Dirr attended the University of Louisville where she earned a BA in English with a focus on creative writing and an MA in Teaching Secondary Education. She has been a secondary educator for 7 years. She has returned to teaching after staying home until all of her children were school aged. They attend Piner Elementary and Twenhofel Middle School. This is Mrs. Dirr's first year with the Academy where she teaches Accelerated English I, Accelerated English II and co-teaches English III with Alyssa Leimenstoll in the morning Engineering Academy. In the afternoon, you may find her subbing across the county.

Brian Noll has been teaching with the Academies for five years. He serves as the Dean of Scholar Affairs and teaches courses in Accelerated Geometry and Algebra 2. Mr. Noll holds a B.S. in Mathematics Education, a M.Ed. in Teacher as Leader, and is working on an Ed.D. in Educational Leadership. He is a Google Level 1 and 2 certified educator and is certified to teach AP Calculus AB and PLTW Biomedical Sciences. His favorite lesson to teach is the transformation of functions and unit circle. When he is not at school, he enjoys traveling with his family to explore the world.
